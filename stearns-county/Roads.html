Section 1 Overview
Originator Stearns County
Title Roads
Abstract The road centerline file for Stearns County was developed in three phases:

Phase 1: 1998- GeoComm, Inc was hired to utilize the base centerline file from Minnesota Department of Transportation (MNDOT). GeoComm assigned MSAG valid street names and address ranges to the existing line segments. GeoComm did not adjust the positional accuracy of the road segments. The current Master Street Address Guide (MSAG) and the Stearns County Emergency Atlas were utilized as resources for the assignment of the street names and address ranges. No field verification was done or missing road segments added. An additional polygon data set depicting the Emergency Service Zones was developed based on the MSAG reference and resource hard copy maps provided by the county. The data sets are in ESRI shape file format.

Phase 2: In 1999, Stearns County contracted with The Lawrence Group to complete phase 2 of the county's E-911 roads mapping. This phase required the mapping vendor to spatially adjust existing roads to the right-of-way in the completed portions of the Stearns County Parcel Data set, add missing road segments, verify naming conventions, and verify that all roads were snapped together. This phase also required the addition of attributes require by the county's new Computer-Aided-Dispatch software

Phase 3: Nov, 2002. GeoComm, Inc was contracted to complete the spatial adjustment of the road centerline to the positional accuracy of the Stearns County parcel map data (See Metadata for the parcel data for accuracy level). Stearns County provided the current parcel map data, current road centerline, boundary file, Emergency Atlases with Emergency Service Zones, current Master Street Address Guide, 911 data base possibly from 2000, and aerial photos for resources. The road centerline segments were adjusted within the right of way on the parcel layer. If the right of way could not be identified, the air photo was reviewed to determine the location of the segment(s). If the location of the road could not be determined from the aerial photos, the road was field verified and GPS was used. GeoComm did not compare the address ranges in the parcel data to all street segments. If the errors were located in the certain address ranges from the parcel data through another process, GeoComm corrected the address ranges.
Purpose Stearns county E-9-1-1 system for use within a dispatch mapping system that will locate wireline/wireless E911 calls and other GIS applications as well as to allow for the integration of relational data sets.

A. - Feature class codes (f_class) are generalized versions of the U.S. census bureau standards. Namely: Typical Interstates are labeled A10, Interstate HOV lanes are labeled A15 (new with Dec. '98 release), US highways A20, State highways A25, County Roads A30, City Streets A40, Ramps and Loops A63, Service Drives A64, Private Roads A66, Dormitory Walkways A71, and Restricted Access A98. As of release 2.2b (a mid-quarter update on August 5, 1998), US highways and State Highways were split into two distinct feature class codes of A20 (US Highways) and A25 (State Highways).

B. - RD_CODE (road code) 1 -- State; 3 -- County Road; 5 -- Township Road; 6 -- City Road
